About Google

 Google is one of the world’s leading technology companies, founded in September 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University. The duo developed a search engine called "Backrub," which utilized a novel ranking algorithm called PageRank to prioritize web pages based on their relevance and authority. Renamed Google—a play on the mathematical term "googol" (1 followed by 100 zeros)—the search engine aimed to "organize the world’s information and make it universally accessible and useful." Google's initial success came from its clean interface and superior search results, quickly surpassing competitors. The company’s 2004 IPO marked its rise to one of the most influential tech giants, and its mission expanded beyond search to encompass various digital products and services.

 Over the years, Google diversified its offerings, including Gmail (2004), Google Maps (2005), YouTube (acquired in 2006), and Android (acquired in 2005), which became the world’s most popular mobile operating system. It pioneered innovations in advertising with Google Ads, which became its primary revenue stream. In 2015, Google restructured under the parent company Alphabet Inc., reflecting its ventures into fields like artificial intelligence, self-driving cars, and healthcare. Despite its dominance, Google has faced controversies over data privacy, antitrust concerns, and its influence over digital ecosystems. Today, it remains a cornerstone of the internet, shaping how billions access and interact with information.
